Fort Worth Light Rail Feasibility & Connectivity Analysis

Independent Project, 2026

A GIS-based transit demand and light rail feasibility analysis for the City of Fort Worth and Trinity Metro. The project examines Fort Worth's significant mobility gap — a car-dependent city with disconnected cultural and economic hubs — and proposes a framework for reconnecting them through rail. Deliverables include a spatial demand analysis using demographic data and existing bus corridors, a hub connectivity analysis modeling optimal routes, static maps, and an interactive web map for city and community stakeholders.

Denver RTD Transit Network — Web App & Experience Builder Comparison

Lab Analysis, 2026

A comparative data visualization exercise building two interactive representations of the Denver RTD light rail and commuter rail network — one using a custom ArcGIS Maps SDK JavaScript web application, and one using ArcGIS Experience Builder. The lab evaluates both approaches across speed of development, visual output quality, and degree of design control, ultimately finding that the coded interface produced a cleaner, more customizable result while the Experience Builder offered a faster no-code entry point.

Fort Worth Urban Infill Site Analysis

Lab Analysis, 2026

A multi-criteria GIS analysis identifying viable urban infill parcels in Fort Worth using a composite suitability model. Variables include improvement-to-land value ratio (identifying underutilized parcels), average household income by census tract, proximity to city parks (half-mile buffer), and clustering of multi-family code violations (indicating latent housing demand). The analysis targets areas where infill development would be both economically viable and responsive to observed community need.

Chicago Industry Location Quotient Analysis

Lab Analysis, 2026

A spatial analysis of industry clustering across four sectors in the Chicago metropolitan area using location quotients — a measure of industry concentration relative to a broader benchmark. The analysis examines how Transportation, Arts & Entertainment, Professional Services, and Manufacturing firms agglomerate around key logistical nodes, demographic centers, and infrastructure corridors, and considers the implications of those patterns for Chicago's regional economy.

Elderly Infrastructure Site Analysis: North Central Texas

Professional Assignment, 2024

A GIS-based site suitability analysis identifying the four highest-concentration zones of elderly population (>65) in North Central Texas — evaluating each site against a weighted infrastructure index including hospitals, urgent care, fire and police stations, shopping centers, parks, and highway access. Spatial Distribution of Drinking Establishments in the Dallas–Fort Worth Metroplex

Lab Analysis, 2026

An analysis of the spatial logic driving the location of four distinct types of drinking establishments across the Dallas–Fort Worth metroplex — craft breweries, wineries, wine bars, and Applebee's. The analysis examines how each business type reflects a different set of site selection criteria, from demographic targeting and land input requirements to franchise coverage strategies, and maps these patterns across four cartographic layouts to highlight contrasts in concentration and distribution.
